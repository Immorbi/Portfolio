Poster collection for The Gallery

A series of posters designed for The Gallery — an independent contemporary art space in Tbilisi. Each poster accompanies an exhibition, lecture, or pop-up event, held together by a flexible visual system that lets every show speak in its own voice.

Year 2025 — 2026
Goal

The Gallery runs a dense program — exhibitions, lectures, pop-ups, fashion showcases — each with its own tone and mood. The task was to give all of it a unified face without flattening what makes each event distinct.

Task

Design nine event posters for an independent contemporary art space in Tbilisi. Each poster needed to feel right for its specific show while still reading as part of one ongoing series.

Solution

I built the system around small, consistent anchors: a corner logo, a fixed information block, a shared way of treating dates and names. Inside that frame, every poster was free to look like the show it announces — different typefaces, different image logic, different palettes.

01 — Context

A gallery that changes its voice every month.

The Gallery runs a dense program of exhibitions, lectures and pop-ups. Each event has its own tone — a textile group show, a philosophy lecture series, a fashion showcase — and a single locked-down identity would flatten everything that makes the program interesting.

Instead, the system is held together by small, consistent anchors: a tiny corner logo, a recurring information block, and a shared way of framing dates and names. Inside that frame, every poster is free to look like the show it announces.

02 — Approach

One system, eight voices.

Each poster starts from the subject: a textile exhibition leans on hands and sky; a lecture on anarchism in art borrows the aesthetic of a printed pamphlet; a glass-sculpture show breathes through motion blur and chroma.

Typography does the heavy lifting — contrasting display treatments carry the mood, while a quiet sans-serif keeps the factual information legible and consistent across the series.
