DIRTALK Brandbook

A visual identity system for DIRTALK, a media project centered on women in the industry. The brandbook brings together logo, color, type and post formats into one clear system that can stretch across covers, quotes and everyday content without losing its voice.

01 — Premise

A media identity built around voice, presence and recognition.

DIRTALK needed a system that could hold interviews, announcements, quotes and community-led content without slipping into generic media branding. The identity had to feel direct, contemporary and easy to recognise at a glance.

The solution was to build the brand around language itself: bold headlines, isolated phrases, monospaced typography and a symbol system that enters the layout the way a strong quote enters a conversation.

04 — Logo

The logotype feels friendly, but still has character. The upper part is strict and geometric, while the lower half softens into rounder forms, as if the mark is inviting the viewer into conversation.

The small star at the end works like a light accent, a signal that there is something special here. Together, the mark feels clear, confident and slightly ironic, without becoming too polished or distant.

The brand should feel like a statement on the page: unmistakable, emotionally legible and easy to reuse without losing its edge.
Brand direction note
04 — Covers

Two cover directions built from the same system.

Alongside the core identity, I explored how the system behaves on covers. Each direction leans on a different visual metaphor, but both stay inside the same language of color, spacing and statement-led composition.

Collective voice

Several silhouettes overlap into one shared presence. Different figures remain visible, but together they read as one strong image about solidarity, visibility and collective voice.

Speaker as image

Here the focus shifts to a host-like figure, making the visual feel more personal and media-facing. The contrast between green, pink and black keeps the tone playful, but still sharp and recognisable.

05 — Post design

Several post formats were developed inside the same branding system.

The system also includes a set of post layouts for different tones and content types. Each one uses the same visual grammar, while shifting the balance between typography, character imagery and abstract form.
